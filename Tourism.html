Welcome to Ludhiana

Ludhiana district is one of the 22 districts in the state of Punjab in North-West Republic of India. Ludhiana is the largest city in the state. The city stands on the Sutlej River's old bank, 13 km south of its present course. Ludhiana is located 100 km west from state capital Chandigarh on NH 95 and is centrally located on National Highway 1 from Indian capital New Delhi to Amritsar, and is well connected to New Delhi by road, frequent train service and by air. Residents of the city may be referred to as Ludhianvis. Ludhianvis own maximum number of luxury cars in Punjab.

The main industries are bicycle parts and hosiery.Apart from being an industrial town, Ludhiana is home to several places of tourist interest that draws in a large number of touristsfrom various parts of India. Tourism in Ludhiana is flourishing and gaining popularity and it is now an important stopover in the itinerary of the tourist who is on his tour of Punjab. It is an important pilgrim center of Punjab and you will come across lots of temples, sacred shrines and Gurudwaras located all over the city.

Ludhiana gets its name from the Lodhi Dynasty, which is believed to have founded the city in 1480. During the reign of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, Ludhiana became an important British cantonment. According to the 1901 census, Hindus numbered 269,076, or 40 per cent of the total; Muslims, 235,937, or 35 per cent; and Sikhs, 164,919, or 24 per cent. In 1947 due to violence and strife between the communities, the most of the Muslim population left for Pakistan.
